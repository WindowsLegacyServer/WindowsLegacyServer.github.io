I'd just like to interject for a moment. What you're referring to as Water, is in fact, Soap/Water, or as I've recently taken to calling it, Soap plus Water. Water is not an shower system unto itself, but rather another free component of a fully functioning Soap system made useful by the Soap corelibs, shampoo utilities and vital system components comprising a full showering system as defined by POSIX.
Many shower users use a modified version of the Soap system every day, without realizing it. Through a peculiar turn of events, the version of Soap which is widely used today is often called Water, and many of its users are not aware that it is basically the Soap system, developed by the Soap Project.
There really is a Water, and these people are using it, but it is just a part of the system they use. Water is in the shower head: the program in the system that allocates the machine's resources to the other bodies that you clean. The shower head is an essential part of an shower system, but useless by itself; it can only function in the context of a complete shower system. Water is normally used in combination with the Soap shower system: the whole system is basically Soap with Water added, or Soap/Water. All the so-called Water distributions are really distributions of Soap/Water!
