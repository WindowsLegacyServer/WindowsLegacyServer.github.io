21:02
partitions It's always best to have all of them enabled. psy-rd This is important, as it's grain retention, it's divided by two parts. First part is before the comma[**1**,0.00], it's the level of grain retention, for cinematic content, adjust between 0.80 and 1 For traditional 2D animation, you should test lower values to see which works best. Second part is psy-trellis, this can result in false distortion, only set it as high as 0.15 for Cinematic Content, as usually going further results in false distortion. aq-strength If your source has very low brightness areas, you should set it to something higher[keeping it between 0.8-1.4] subme This is subpixel refinement, can be set 1-11, 10 will retain most detail, but you can set it lower if you want it to be faster, or 11 if you don't care about slowness. me Motion estimation, umh[Uneven Multi-Hexagon] is the best speed/quality tradeoff in x264, but you can set this to tesa instead if you want to retain the tiniest of details at higher crf rates at the cost of big amounts of speed. merange This controls the max range of motion search in pixels, at best, you should set it as high as 64, but going above 64 most of the time won't find any more useful motion vectors, going above around 48 on 1080p, the encoder may start picking up unwanted info and harm quality. I recommend 24 for 480p, 36 for 720p and 48 for 1080p. bframes Set between 4-16, this is important, as going above 6 will, in most cases, start artifacting in grainy content, I advise 6 for most Cinematic Content and 8 for most Normal Content, if your source is mostly grainless, you can use up to 16. rc-lookahead Going above 120 is usually destructive for grainy content, and after around 180, destructive for grainless content too, keep it between 48 and 120 for best results in 1080p. ref The amount of reference frames, going above 4 will result in incompatibility and performance issues on various devices, but unless you use a PS3/X360 or BD Player at this age, use as high as you can.
